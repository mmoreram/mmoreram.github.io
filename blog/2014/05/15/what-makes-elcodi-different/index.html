What Makes Elcodi Different?

May 15th, 2014 | Comments

Well, the league of web development, in which we play, is a fussy league, I started to recieve the first questions about the project I find myself developing… ¿ Why a new project called Elcodi wants to cover the needs of ecommerce when there is something similar called Sylius?

Although I twitt that I would write a comparison between both platforms, I firmly believe that it is absurd to compare both of them, since Sylius is a very mature project and has lots of features we do not have ( nor will we have short-term ). So, this post is nothing more than a declaration of intentions about why we have decided to start a separated project from the existing options.

Firstly, I want to mention my complete respect to Sylius. It’s a project builded from many different contributors, and I do not want to compare their results with our ambition at all. I think both are two projects with two different perspectives, and since one of our most important value ​​is humility as a team, we start as a small project. Ambitious, but for now, small.

Secondly, I want to talk with the community through what are the three basic paws in our project. All three are equally important and we strongly believe that one feeds the other. We are very strict on these three aspects, and it will remain as it is throughout the years.

Event-Driven Design

Elcodi is a completely event-oriented project. All components have a set of events, especially designed to customize the behavior of a specific action.

Without giving importance to this point means that many people will have to fork the project itself to implement their behavior, and this is not a good philosophy. A third component should behave as such, giving capacity as high as possible to be customized.

Clean Code

Our first and most important objective is to provide developers with a set of useful tools for working their own implementations. We do not claim to be the idyllic and immediate solution for all existing projects, but our goal, more or less realistic, is to provide a completely customizable to those recurring problems in all projects style solution.

For example, we can not solve all management Carta for obvious reasons: Each project has its own unique business needs. What we can do is to propose a simple common behavior, thus, giving the opportunity to evolve offering a completely customizable domain model, giving the opportunity to extend the behavior with proposed events and offering a decent and growing documentation.

In my view, and because realistically, the real needs are demanded by a host of different profiles, we take care of our programming style, designing clean code, standardizing styles and understanding everything we do. We do not position ourselves in relation to any extreme programming philosophy, always chasing the latest trends. We just want to create a nice project, both for developers who want something small and stable as for those who believe that the project will grow much.

Projection executive

We are a technology project, but first and foremost we are a company. This means that even we have aspiration to be fully open source, we have a business model behind, defined by a financial structure and economic forecasting constantly growing. As you can see on our website, our key role in the process is the Market. One of the great advantages that event-driven architecture gives us is that it can fit very easily as a decoupled processing module.

Actually, the point of this distinctive feature is that we do not base the development of the project only on “free time reviewers” but professional people will be responsible for developing the project, to train people interested and manage the quality of extensions that appear on the market. A very positive point about the scheme.

At the same time, the developer who wants to base its implementation on Elcodi will have minimal confidence that there will probably be modules as solutions to their needs. At least this is our projection, a complete win-win.

Final thoughts

I repeat. In no time we want to compete with anyone. I understand that every project has its trajectory. Ours is this. The collaboration between projects is essential, and we have a strong commitment to work to collaborate with other projects, because at last, after all, a win-win all :)

Coming soon we will draw a public roadmap, as well as a set of training modules for people who want to learn some tidbits of web architecture and want to know a little more about our project.

Obviously, if you want to know about project’s updates, you can follow our activities in Twitter – @elcodi_dev or follow our development in Github – @elcodi. Obviously we want people to try, question, discuss constructively and give us reviews on our implementations. The contributions and contributors to the project will be well received and appreciated :)
